AUTOMATING EIA PROCESS USING AI

Utilizing GIS and Remote Sensing for Enhanced Environmental Impact Assessment

Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) is a systematic process used to evaluate the potential environmental effects of a proposed project or development before decisions are made to move forward with the project. The aim of EIA is Environmental Protection, Sustainable Development, Regulatory Compliance, Informed Decision-Making, Risk Management, Public Participation and Transparency, Cost Saving and Improving Project Design. Traditional methods of conducting EIA are generally qualitative and less precise, relying on expert judgment and simpler, straightforward approaches. These methods may overlook complex interactions and often involve limited public participation, being primarily expert-driven. They tend to be more descriptive and less predictive, focusing on identifying impacts rather than predicting them. Additionally, traditional methods offer limited temporal and spatial analysis, which is often static. Hence the need for an advanced approach for EIA which involves utilization of GIS and remote sensing technologies. This approach is effective for spatial data management and analysis while facilitating the integration of various data types. These methods provide up-to-date and comprehensive environmental data, particularly valuable for large and inaccessible areas. They allow for detailed prediction and analysis, capable of modeling complex interactions. Furthermore, modern EIA methods enhance stakeholder engagement and the integration of local knowledge, thereby improving transparency. They offer a comprehensive and holistic approach, identifying cumulative impacts and ultimately enhancing the quality of decision-making through the integration of diverse data and analysis

To demonstrate how use of GIS and remote sensing techniques can be applied to improve the environmental impact assessment process.
To develop a GIS-based Decision Support System that can analyze complex environmental data, predict impacts and develop mitigation strategies in Environmental Impact assessment.
